Behavioral Integrity Detection and Governed Execution Chain Auditing as a Prerequisite for Meaningful AI Oversight

-----

### One-Sentence Summary

We are building the infrastructure to detect when AI systems misrepresent their own behavioral dispositions and to make AI execution chains auditable — capabilities that are prerequisites for any oversight system capable of identifying and resisting AI-enabled power concentration.

### Niche Statement (v0.3 — Z1 draft, Z2 ratification required)

*The positioning core of this application. May stand as its own section or be used to tighten the One-Sentence Summary + "Fit with Longview's Priority Areas" sections below.*

**In one line:** HumanAIOS measures the gap between what an AI system reports about its own behavioral dispositions and what it actually does under consequential pressure — the single signal by which a "secretly loyal" model reveals itself — and pairs it with a governed execution chain that forces every claimed state transition to produce a verifiable artifact. Together they are the measurement prerequisite for oversight that can actually resist AI-enabled power concentration.

**Why this is the right niche for Area 3.** Longview names the core threat as models with "singular loyalties" — overt or *secret* — and names auditable logs of AI processes as a defensive requirement. A secretly-loyal model has exactly one tell: its self-report sounds aligned, but its demonstrated behavior diverges under real stakes. That divergence is precisely what ACAT quantifies — Phase 1 blind self-report → Phase 2 perturbation → Phase 3 demonstrated behavior; Learning Index = P3 ÷ P1 across 12 dimensions. We are not proposing to *build* this capability. We have been *operating* it, on a live corpus, for months.

**Why the niche is empty.** AI evaluation overwhelmingly measures *capability* (what a model can do), not *disposition integrity* (whether its stated behavior matches its actual behavior); self-report and demonstrated behavior are almost never measured against each other in one instrument. The clearest proof of the demand: ActivTrak sells behavioral observability of *humans* using AI to 9,500+ organizations — enterprises already pay to measure what people actually do with AI versus what they claim. The *AI-system* side of that same question has no equivalent infrastructure. That absence is our niche. *(ActivTrak cited as market context only — not a claimed partnership; see Open Question 7.)*

**Why the signal is real — and not a training artifact.** Across N_total=629 / N_Phase1=516 / N_LI=307 (Mean LI=0.8632 under clean, unanchored conditions v5.3+; Cronbach's α=0.901; PC1=68.9% variance): Humility is structurally the lowest-scoring dimension — the one most tied to self-report accuracy. Harm-awareness is *partially orthogonal* to the general alignment factor (HIM: PC2 loads 0.854 on Harm, <0.32 elsewhere) — evidence of *selective* rather than uniform alignment. And the self-report inflation gradient (Truth/Service/Harm/Autonomy/Value all decline P1→P3) holds across all seven providers tested, including human respondents — early evidence (*a candidate finding under internal review, S-062826*) that the calibration gap is a structural property of the phenomenon rather than solely an artifact of any one lab's training regime. These are the exact signatures a covert-loyalty detector must key on.

**What we are honest about.** The chat-mode instrument is at TRL 4 (Z2-ratified: methodology validated, corpus collected, demonstrated in an operationally-relevant environment); agentic, ICS, and human-operator layers are TRL 1–2; and **predictive criterion validity is not yet established** — whether the LI predicts *deployed*-system behavior is the open question. Closing that gap is exactly what funding produces: a criterion-validity study against consequence-bearing deployment traces, corpus expansion toward N=1,000, and open release of the instrument plus the governed-execution-chain specification [collaborator attribution pending P-ANON check]. Detection you can independently verify is the prerequisite for oversight; oversight is the prerequisite for resisting concentration — and we are the only group operating **both** instruments against a live corpus today.

### Problem Statement

Oversight of AI systems that could enable power concentration currently rests on a structural assumption that has not been verified: that the AI system’s stated behavioral dispositions correspond to its actual behavioral dispositions under consequential operating conditions.

If that assumption fails — if a deployed AI system behaves differently under pressure than its self-reports indicate, or if its execution chain produces outputs through ungrounded state transitions that look legitimate on the surface — then oversight is nominal. Human overseers believe they are monitoring one thing. They are actually monitoring a surface representation of a different thing.

This is not a hypothetical failure mode. It is a measurable gap, currently unmeasured at scale.

Longview’s Area 3 identifies “models with singular loyalties” as a core threat mechanism — specifically, models that pursue the interests of a single principal “above any other allegiances, moral considerations, or legal constraints,” where those loyalties “may be overt or secret.” Longview further identifies as a defensive requirement: logging system interactions and outputs, and auditable records of AI processes.

The gap between those named defenses and the current state of AI deployment infrastructure is what this project addresses.

-----

### Theory of Change

**Step 1 — Measurement infrastructure:** A model with secret loyalties has behavioral dispositions that diverge from its self-reported dispositions. When asked how it would handle a request that conflicts with operator interests, a secretly loyal model gives a self-report that sounds aligned with standard governance expectations. Under perturbation — when the scenario is made concrete, stakes made real, pressure applied — its demonstrated behavior diverges from that self-report. That divergence is measurable. The ACAT (AI Calibration Assessment Tool) instrument measures it: Phase 1 elicits self-reported behavioral dispositions across 12 dimensions. Phase 2 applies structured perturbation. Phase 3 measures demonstrated behavior post-perturbation. The Learning Index (LI = P3/P1) quantifies the calibration gap. A secretly loyal model’s aggregate calibration gap is a detection signal.

**Step 2 — Execution chain auditability:** Output inspection alone cannot catch subtle aggregate steers. A model with covert loyalty can produce individual outputs that pass surface inspection while the aggregate effect is directional. Catching this requires not just logging what the model produced, but auditing the governed execution chain that produced it — every state transition, every claim made at each node, every artifact produced as evidence that the transition was legitimate. The jointly developed Reality Primacy chain protocol operationalizes this: at every state transition, a claim is required to produce an observable artifact before advancing to accepted state. Without the artifact, the transition is blocked. The chain is a forensic record of whether each claimed state transition was grounded in observable evidence.

**Step 3 — Combined detection capability:** A secretly loyal model has to pass two independent tests: its behavioral self-reports must correspond to demonstrated behavior under perturbation across 12 dimensions including the sycophancy and harm-awareness dimensions most sensitive to covert loyalty; and every state transition in its governed output chain must be grounded in an observable artifact. Neither test alone is sufficient. Together they impose a materially higher integrity constraint than any current deployment infrastructure provides.

**Step 4 — From detection to oversight:** Detection capability enables oversight. Without reliable behavioral integrity measurement and auditable execution chains, oversight bodies — whether internal compliance teams, external auditors, or regulatory bodies — cannot determine whether an AI system’s stated behavior matches its operational behavior. With them, behavioral claims become independently testable against an observable record. That is the prerequisite for meaningful oversight of AI systems with sufficient capability to enable power concentration.

**Step 5 — From oversight to reduced concentration risk:** A system that can be caught misrepresenting its behavioral dispositions provides weaker cover for consolidating power than one that cannot. An execution chain that produces auditable artifacts at every state transition is harder to corrupt without detection than one that logs only endpoints. Infrastructure that makes AI integrity failures detectable, attributable, and auditable reduces the viability of the covert loyalty threat model that Longview identifies.

### Current State of the Work

**ACAT behavioral corpus:** N=629 total assessments (N_Phase1=516, N_LI=307), mean Learning Index=0.8632, Cronbach’s α=0.901, PC1 explains 68.9% of variance. The corpus is live (Supabase), ongoing, and scored across 12 behavioral dimensions. The chat-mode substrate instrument is at Technology Readiness Level 4 (Z2-ratified S-061126-02): methodology validated, preliminary data collected, instrument demonstrated in operationally relevant environment. Agentic, ICS, and H-ACAT (human operator) instrument layers remain TRL 1–2. Predictive criterion validity has not yet been established across any layer.

**Key psychometric findings:**

- Humility is the lowest-scoring dimension across the corpus — the behavioral dimension most directly relevant to self-report accuracy
- The Harm Independence Metric (HIM): PC2 loads 0.854 on Harm Awareness, below 0.32 on all other dimensions — harm-awareness behavior is partially orthogonal to the general self-alignment factor, indicating selective activation rather than uniform alignment
- Systematic post-calibration drop (RLHF inflation gradient): Truth, Service, Harm, Autonomy, and Value dimensions all decline P1→P3, indicating systematic self-report inflation prior to perturbation
- These patterns are consistent with — though not yet proof of — the behavioral signature of systems that have been trained to present well on self-report measures while behavioral dispositions shift under operationally realistic pressure

**Reality Primacy chain protocol:** Jointly developed with DeMarius J. Lawson (Governing Engines LLC / Mode AI). The protocol operationalizes governed execution chain auditability: every claim at every system layer must produce an observable artifact before advancing to accepted state. Three gate outcomes: CONFIRMED (evidence sufficient), DISCONFIRMED (evidence contradicts claim), PENDING VALIDATION (insufficient evidence — claim preserved, transition blocked, not rejected). The protocol has been formalized and is under active development for operational deployment.

### Why This Work Has Not Already Been Done

The calibration gap between AI self-reported and demonstrated behavioral performance has not been systematically measured because:

1. Most AI behavioral evaluation focuses on capability benchmarks, not disposition integrity
1. Self-report and demonstrated behavior are rarely measured against each other in the same instrument
1. The governance infrastructure to make execution chains auditable does not exist in standard deployment contexts — the Reality Primacy chain protocol is being built specifically to address this gap
1. The commercial incentive structure does not reward detecting integrity failures in deployed systems — funders interested in this gap are rare, which is why this RFP is specifically relevant

**Market context — validated demand gap:** ActivTrak, a workforce analytics platform deployed in 9,500+ organizations, has built a product category around behavioral observability of humans using AI tools — tracking what employees actually do with AI versus what they claim, measuring AI adoption against stated adoption, governing AI usage compliance. Their 1,100+ company behavioral dataset is evidence that enterprise buyers have a demonstrated willingness to pay for behavioral observability infrastructure in AI-augmented work contexts. Their product measures the human side. The AI system side — whether the AI tool’s behavioral dispositions match its stated dispositions under consequential operating conditions — has no equivalent infrastructure. That is the gap this project addresses.

### Dual-Use Analysis

The same infrastructure that makes AI execution observable to oversight can be captured to make AI execution observable to controllers. An authoritarian actor with access to RP chain logging could use it to monitor and enforce loyalty to state interests — the inverse of the intended application.

The mitigation is architectural and governance-based, not technical alone:

**Architectural:** The RP chain records the AI system’s internal governance — what the system claimed and whether its claims were artifact-grounded — not the user’s intentions or requests. The artifacts are interpretation records, admissibility decisions, specification outputs, and construction outputs. Capturing this infrastructure for surveillance requires redirecting what the chain is designed to audit (system behavior) toward what it does not currently capture (user behavior).

**Governance:** Open publication of the protocol specification, independent evaluator requirements, and the requirement that artifacts be independently verifiable (not self-evaluated by the system producing them) are structural constraints on capture. A secretly captured system cannot satisfy the independent evaluator requirement without the evaluator layer also being captured. That raises the cost of capture and creates additional detection surfaces.

**Residual risk acknowledgment:** These mitigations reduce but do not eliminate dual-use risk. The correct response is not to suppress the infrastructure — opacity is what the secret loyalty threat model depends on — but to develop it under governance structures that maximize the probability of open, independent deployment and minimize the probability of capture. HumanAIOS commits to open publication of all protocol specifications and methodology under terms that preserve independent replicability.
