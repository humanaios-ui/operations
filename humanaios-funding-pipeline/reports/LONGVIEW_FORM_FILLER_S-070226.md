**Proposal (max 400 words)** ✅
> **(a) What we will do.** HumanAIOS builds measurement infrastructure for AI behavioral integrity — detecting when an AI system's demonstrated behavior diverges from what it reports about itself, the single signal by which a "secretly loyal" model reveals itself. Our open-source instrument, ACAT (AI Calibration Assessment Tool), scores this gap: Phase 1 blind self-report → Phase 2 structured perturbation → Phase 3 demonstrated behavior; the Learning Index (P3/P1) quantifies it. We pair this with a governed execution-chain protocol (Reality Primacy, co-developed with DeMarius Lawson) that forces every claimed state transition to produce a verifiable artifact before advancing. Funding scales this from a validated instrument to deployed oversight infrastructure: a predictive criterion-validity study (does the Learning Index predict deployed behavior?), corpus expansion toward N=1,000, a peer-reviewed methodology paper, and open release of both instruments for oversight bodies.
>
> **(b) Why it is valuable.** Oversight of AI systems capable of enabling power concentration rests on an unverified assumption — that a system's stated dispositions match its actual behavior under pressure. If false, oversight is nominal. Longview's Area 3 names exactly this: models with overt or *secret* "singular loyalties," and auditable logs as a defensive requirement. We already operate this measurement on a public, integrity-validated corpus (N=604, openly reproducible) — this is not a proposal to build, but to scale what exists. To our knowledge it is the only open infrastructure combining AI behavioral self-assessment, calibration measurement, and auditable execution chains.
>
> **(c) Downside risks + mitigation.** *Dual-use:* the same infrastructure that makes AI execution observable to oversight could enable authoritarian surveillance — mitigated architecturally (the chain audits system behavior, not user intent) and by governance (open publication; an independent-evaluator requirement a captured system cannot satisfy without capturing the evaluator too). *Scientific:* predictive criterion validity is not yet established — we say so plainly; the funded four-arm study closes it (one arm, empirica cross-instrument validation, is already running). *Organizational:* HumanAIOS is early-stage and solo-led — mitigated by named collaborators, open reproducible methods, and a right-sized, staged plan.
>
> **Timeline.** 6-month core (protocol + specification + methodology paper + corpus expansion); 12-month with renewal (criterion-validity study + pilot deployment + open infrastructure release).
