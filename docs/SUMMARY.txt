THE ANONYMITY PARADOX SOLVED
─────────────────────────────

WITHOUT SSI (Current):
  Rater generates: anon-a7f3b2c1 (opaque token)
  ✓ Perfect anonymity (cannot reverse to person)
  ✗ No credibility binding (anyone could be "anon-a7f3b2c1")
  ✗ No portability (token only valid in HumanAIOS)

WITH SSI (Proposed):
  Rater generates keypair: Ed25519 (private key stored securely)
  Rater gets DID: did:key:z6MkhaXg... (deterministic from public key)
  
  Stores in system:
  • rater_did (PUBLIC)
  • public_key_base58 (PUBLIC, non-secret)
  • capabilities: ["acat_scorer_v1"] (PUBLIC, generic)
  • rater_affiliation: "researcher" (OPTIONAL, generic, no PII)
  
  ✓ Pseudonymous identity (DID, not name)
  ✓ Credibility portable ("This DID scored 47 assessments with 0.89 agreement")
  ✓ Cryptographic non-repudiation (signature proves "this DID made this assessment")
  ✓ Personal identity severable (Jane never submits name; only DID is public)
  ✓ Anonymity STRENGTHENED (fingerprint ≠ identity; cannot correlate)
  ✓ Portability (DID works on any SSI-capable system)

RATIONALE FOR THIS WORK
────────────────────────

Core insight from your observation:
  "The SSI from the human side would strengthen our anonymity capabilities."

Why this is true:
  • Current: Rater anonymity is strong (opaque token) but unverifiable
  • With SSI: Rater credibility becomes portable without revealing identity
  • Result: "What the rater did" (DID) ≠ "Who the rater is" (person)
  • Consequence: Stronger anonymity + verifiable expertise

This solves a real gap:
  • AI fingerprinting is strong (provider:model)
  • Human anonymity is strong (anon-{12hex})
  • BUT chain of custody between them is currently unverifiable
  • SSI makes the chain verifiable without breaking anonymity
