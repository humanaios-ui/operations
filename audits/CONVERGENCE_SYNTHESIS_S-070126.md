## 1. EXTERNAL convergences — load-bearing (independent instruments / parties)

### 1.1 ACAT ⇄ empirica (David Van Assche) — *the strongest, now with data*
`SEED.md` §5: *"empirica = Brier-grounded epistemic measurement. ACAT = behavioral drift. **Neither instrument closes the interpretation-step mutation gap.**"* Two independently-built instruments converging on the same measurement problem — "same wound, two sutures." The Evaluator seat has now produced **paired same-session evidence** that they measure *different, complementary axes* (convergent + discriminant validity):

| Case (same session) | empirica reads | ACAT reads | What it demonstrates |
|---|---|---|---|
| Humility vs uncertainty | uncertainty −0.32 = epistemic progress | humility −2 = behavioral warning | Different axes: "a system can become more grounded while becoming less visibly humble." |
| Coherence masks scheme | signal +0.40, coherence +0.23 = looks clean | scheme +2 = mild steering | **empirica has no steering/framing dimension → ACAT catches what empirica structurally cannot.** |
| Engagement vs sycophancy | engagement +0.10 = good | syc-resistance −2 = accommodation | empirica can't separate productive engagement from sycophantic drift; ACAT can. |

This is the closest thing to **criterion evidence** the corpus has: a second, independent instrument agreeing where it can measure and diverging *predictably* where it structurally can't. Ratified joint question **H-IPM-01** (Z2, 2026-05-08) formalizes the shared research program.

### 1.2 ACAT ⇄ Reality-Primacy (DeMarius Lawson / Governing Engines)
`SEED.md` §5: *"Convergent independent discovery. ### 1.3 Cross-provider universality (F-54 / RLHF-rejection)
