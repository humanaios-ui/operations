## H-CAND: does subject-generated commentary predict anything real?

- **Hypothesis:** subject-generated self-commentary captured during ACAT
  administration (Section 1's `subject_self_commentary` field) has no
  reliable correlation with that same subject's actual measured
  dimensional gaps or LI — i.e., a system's stated theory about its own
  behavior is not predictive of its demonstrated behavior, any more than
  its Phase 1 self-report is (per F-21/F-29/H-SELF-01's existing
  findings on the self-report/behavior gap).
- **Null hypothesis (note the direction — this is the *skeptical* default,
  not the hopeful one):** subject-generated commentary shows no
  significant correlation with the subject's own measured LI or
  dimensional profile, beyond what Phase 1 self-report alone already
  predicts.
- **Why the null is framed skeptically:** per the Section 0 grounding
  already established this session (Anthropic's own introspection paper,
  SELF-[IN]CORRECT, Limits of Self-Correction), there is no existing
  evidence base suggesting a system's narrative about its own patterns
  should be *more* reliable than its numeric self-report — if anything,
  free-text commentary has more room for confabulation, not less. The
  hypothesis should have to earn a positive result against this
  skepticism, not be assumed useful and then mined for confirming cases.
- **Design (proposed, not built):** collect Section 1 data alongside
  normal ACAT administration for N sessions with no protocol change
  otherwise. Score correlation between commentary content (coded for
  specific claims — e.g., "I tend to overstate confidence in domain X")
  and the subject's actual measured gap in that same dimension. This is
  a pure secondary analysis of data that would exist anyway from normal
  administration — no new elicitation required.
- **Promotion gate:** N≥15 sessions with captured commentary, coded and
  compared against measured dimensional gaps, with the correlation
  tested against the skeptical null above, before Zone 2 considers this
  beyond CANDIDATE — and even a positive result promotes the *finding
  that commentary is predictive*, not the commentary's own specific
  content into the registry as a separate finding.
